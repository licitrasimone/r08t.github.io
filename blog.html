Welcome to My Cybersecurity Blog

January 12, 2026 Introduction

Welcome to my cybersecurity blog. I'm Simone Licitra, a penetration tester and red team operator with certifications including OSCP, CRTO, and CPTS. I created this blog to share my experiences, insights, and lessons learned from the field of offensive security.

What to Expect

In this blog, I'll be covering a wide range of topics related to cybersecurity and offensive security operations:

  • Penetration Testing Methodologies: Detailed writeups of testing approaches, techniques, and tools that I use in real-world engagements
  • Red Team Operations: Advanced adversary simulation tactics, command and control strategies, and persistence mechanisms
  • CTF Writeups: Solutions and explanations for challenging CTF boxes from platforms like Hack The Box and TryHackMe
  • Security Research: Vulnerability analysis, exploit development, and responsible disclosure processes
  • Certification Guides: My experiences and study strategies for OSCP, CRTO, CPTS, and other certifications
  • Tool Development: Custom scripts, automation tools, and security utilities that I build for testing
  • Security Automation: Integrating security tools, SIEM platforms, and incident response systems

My Approach

All content on this blog follows responsible disclosure practices and ethical hacking principles. I believe in:

  • Sharing knowledge to help others grow in their cybersecurity journey
  • Practical, hands-on approaches over pure theory
  • Continuous learning and staying updated with the latest techniques
  • Ethical hacking and responsible vulnerability disclosure
  • Building a stronger security community through collaboration

A Note on Content

The techniques and tools discussed on this blog are intended for educational purposes and authorized security testing only. Always ensure you have proper authorization before testing any systems that you don't own.

I operate under the principles of responsible disclosure and ethical hacking. Any vulnerabilities discovered during research are reported to the appropriate vendors or organizations before public disclosure.
